# United States Capitol Police

The United States Capitol Police (USCP) is a federal law enforcement agency responsible for protecting the U.S. Capitol Building, members of Congress, and visitors to the Capitol complex. The agency is tasked with maintaining security and order within the Capitol grounds, conducting background checks on individuals entering the buildings, and responding to emergencies and threats. Capitol Police officers also provide security for large events and demonstrations taking place on Capitol Hill. As a legislative branch entity, the USCP operates under unique legal frameworks and adopted a FOIA-like request process in 2024.

## Notes

The United States Capitol Police is a legislative branch agency and does not operate a public developer API or open data portal. As a legislative branch entity, the USCP is not subject to the federal Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), though in 2024 the agency adopted a FOIA-like process for certain records requests. Public information is available through official press releases and the USCP website.
